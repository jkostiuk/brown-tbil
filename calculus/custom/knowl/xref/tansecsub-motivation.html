Problem A.2.4.

In SectionÂ 5.4, we developed strategies to evaluate integrals of the form \(\displaystyle\int\sin^n{x}\cos^m{x}dx\text{.}\) Similar strategies often work for integrals of the form \(\displaystyle \tan^n{x}dx\text{.}\) Before exploring this further, we need to understand how to evaluate two foundational integrals; this Problem asks you to do just that.

(a)

Explain and demonstrate how to use an appropriate \(u\)-substitution to show that
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\int\tan{x}dx=\ln(\sec{x})+C\text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

Explain why
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\int\sec{x}dx=\ln|\sec{x}+\tan{x}|+C \end{equation*}
by first multiplying and dividing the integrand by \(\sec{x}+\tan{x}\text{.}\)

(c)

While the above trick might seem elusive (because it is!), try using a similar trick to evaluate the integral \(\displaystyle\int\csc{x}dx\text{.}\)
From now on, you may use these results in future problems without having to reproduce them.
