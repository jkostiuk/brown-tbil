Exercise 2. LE2.

(a)

For each of the following matrices, explain why it is not in reduced row echelon form.
🔗
(i)
\begin{equation*} A = \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & -2 \\ 1 & 5 & 0 & -2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
\(A=\left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 0 & 0 & \markedPivot{\textcolor{red}{1}} & 0 & -2 \\ \markedPivot{\textcolor{red}{1}} & 5 & 0 & -2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\) is not in reduced row echelon form because the pivots are not descending to the right.
🔗
🔗
🔗
(ii)
\begin{equation*} B = \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 1 & -6 & 3 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 7 & 14 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
\(B=\left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & -6 & 3 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \markedPivot{\textcolor{red}{7}} & 14 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\) is not in reduced row echelon form because a leading term has a value besides \(1\text{.}\)
🔗
🔗
🔗
(iii)
\begin{equation*} C = \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 1 & 7 & -4 & 1 & 12 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
\(C=\left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & \textcolor{red}{7} & -4 & 1 & 12 \\ 0 & \markedPivot 1 & -1 & 0 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\) is not in reduced row echelon form because there is a non-zero entry above or below a pivot.
🔗
🔗
🔗

(b)

Use technology to find
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{RREF}\left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 4 & 4 & 3 & 18 & -11 \\ -3 & -3 & 1 & -7 & 5 \\ 3 & 3 & 3 & 15 & -9 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 4 & 4 & 3 & 18 & -11 \\ -3 & -3 & 1 & -7 & 5 \\ 3 & 3 & 3 & 15 & -9 \end{array}\right] {\sim} \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & 1 & 0 & 3 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & \markedPivot 1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

(c)

Show step by step how to find
\begin{equation*} \operatorname{RREF}\left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 4 & 4 & 3 & 18 & -11 \\ -3 & -3 & 1 & -7 & 5 \\ 3 & 3 & 3 & 15 & -9 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
🔗
Solution.
\begin{align*} \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 4 & 4 & 3 & 18 & -11 \\ -3 & -3 & 1 & -7 & 5 \\ 3 & 3 & 3 & 15 & -9 \end{array}\right] \stackrel{R_1+R_2\to R_1}{\sim} & \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & 1 & 4 & 11 & -6 \\ -3 & -3 & 1 & -7 & 5 \\ 3 & 3 & 3 & 15 & -9 \end{array}\right]\\ \stackrel{R_2+3R_1\to R_2}{\stackrel{R_3-3R_1\to R_3}{\sim}} & \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & 1 & 4 & 11 & -6 \\ 0 & 0 & 13 & 26 & -13 \\ 0 & 0 & -9 & -18 & 9 \end{array}\right]\\ \stackrel{\frac{1}{13}R_2\to R_2}{\stackrel{\frac{1}{9}R_3\to R_3}{\sim}} & \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & 1 & 4 & 11 & -6 \\ 0 & 0 & \markedPivot 1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & -1 & -2 & 1 \\ \end{array}\right]\\ \stackrel{R_1-4R_2\to R_1}{\stackrel{R_3+R_1\to R_3}{\sim}} & \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} \markedPivot 1 & 1 & 0 & 3 & -2 \\ 0 & 0 & \markedPivot 1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ \end{array}\right] \end{align*}
