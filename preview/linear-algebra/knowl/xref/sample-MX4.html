Exercise 4. MX4.

(a)

Give a \(3 \times 3\) matrix \(C\) that may be used to perform the row operation \(-5 R_1 \to R_1\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer.
\begin{equation*} I = \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right] \stackrel{-5 R_1 \to R_1}{\sim} \left[\begin{array}{ccc} -5 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]=C \end{equation*}

(b)

Give a \(3 \times 3\) matrix \(M\) that may be used to perform the row operation \(R_1 \leftrightarrow R_3\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer.
\begin{equation*} I = \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right] \stackrel{R_1 \leftrightarrow R_3}{\sim} \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]=M \end{equation*}

(c)

Give a \(3 \times 3\) matrix \(P\) that may be used to perform the row operation \(R_3 - 2 R_2 \to R_3\text{.}\)
🔗
Answer.
\begin{equation*} I = \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right] \stackrel{R_3 - 2 R_2 \to R_3}{\sim}\left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -2 & 1 \end{array}\right]=P \end{equation*}

(e)

Show how to manually apply those row operations to \(A= \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & -3 & -3 \\ 2 & -6 & -5 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right]\text{,}\) then use technology to verify that your matrix in the previous task gives the same result.
🔗
Answer.
\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & -3 & -3 \\ 2 & -6 & -5 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right] \sim\left[\begin{array}{ccc} -5 & 15 & 15 \\ 2 & -6 & -5 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \sim\left[\begin{array}{ccc} -5 & 15 & 15 \\ 2 & -6 & -5 \\ -4 & 12 & 12 \end{array}\right] \sim\left[\begin{array}{ccc} -4 & 12 & 12 \\ 2 & -6 & -5 \\ -5 & 15 & 15 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
